Social Media Character Limits: The Complete 2026 Guide

Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 6 min

Every social media platform enforces different character limits, and exceeding them means your message gets cut off mid-sentence. This guide covers the exact limits for every major platform so you can craft messages that fit perfectly every time.

Twitter / X

Twitter's iconic 280-character limit applies to regular posts. But the platform has expanded significantly:

  • Regular posts: 280 characters
  • Premium subscribers: 25,000 characters
  • Display name: 50 characters
  • Bio: 160 characters
  • DMs: 10,000 characters

Pro tip: Links count as 23 characters regardless of actual URL length. Images and videos don't count toward the limit.

Instagram

Instagram's limits vary by content type:

  • Captions: 2,200 characters
  • Hashtags: 30 per post (recommended: 5-15)
  • Bio: 150 characters
  • Comments: 2,200 characters
  • Stories text: No official limit (practical limit ~250 characters per sticker)

Only the first 125 characters of captions are visible before the "...more" truncation on feed posts.

Facebook / Meta

Facebook is the most generous with character limits:

  • Status posts: 63,206 characters
  • Comments: 8,000 characters
  • Page name: 75 characters
  • Ad headlines: 40 characters
  • Ad primary text: 125 characters (recommended)

Despite the generous limits, posts under 250 characters tend to get 60% more engagement.

LinkedIn

  • Posts: 3,000 characters
  • Articles: 125,000 characters (title: 100 characters)
  • Headline: 220 characters
  • About/Summary: 2,600 characters

LinkedIn truncates posts after about 210 characters with "see more." Front-load your most compelling content.

TikTok

  • Captions: 2,200 characters
  • Bio: 80 characters
  • Username: 24 characters

YouTube

  • Title: 100 characters (first 70 visible in search)
  • Description: 5,000 characters (first 157 visible without clicking)
  • Comments: 10,000 characters
  • Tags: 500 characters total

Conclusion

Understanding character limits isn't just about avoiding cutoffs — it's about optimizing your message for each platform. Twitter rewards concision, LinkedIn rewards depth, and Instagram rewards visual-first thinking. Match your content length to each platform's culture for maximum engagement.
